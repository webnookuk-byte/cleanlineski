Our Story

Why Clean Line Ski Exists

It Started With a Bad Ski Holiday

Not a bad resort. Not bad snow. A bad experience. Twelve people, three ability levels, three hours of admin per day, and one guide trying to keep everyone happy. Nobody skied well. Nobody really wanted to be there after day three.

Clean Line Ski was built in response to that. The idea is simple: take a small group of people who actually want to ski — properly, all day, in the right terrain — and go and do exactly that. No nannying. No waiting around. No apologies for going hard.

The trips are small by design. Six skiers maximum. That's not a commercial constraint — it's the right number. Big enough for atmosphere, small enough to be nimble. We pick terrain based on what the mountain is doing that day, not what's on a pre-planned itinerary.

Personal Presence

I'm On Every Trip

This isn't an operation where you buy a product and get handed to someone else. I plan the trips, I book the accommodation, and I'm on the hill every single day with the group.

That means if the snowpack shifts overnight, I'm the one making the call at 6am. If a skier is struggling on the terrain, I'm there. If someone wants to push into something bigger, I know what we're getting into.

The personal element isn't a marketing point — it's the whole model. You're not booking a package. You're joining a trip I'd want to do myself.

What We Stand For

Our Values

Small Groups Only

Six skiers maximum. No exceptions. The quality of skiing — the terrain choices, the pace, the experience — depends on keeping numbers tight. We don't compromise on this.

Early Starts

The best snow doesn't last. We're on first lifts, full stop. This isn't the trip for leisurely mornings and long lunches. The mountain rewards those who show up early — we show up early.

Terrain Integrity

We go where the skiing is good, not where it's easy to manage. That means reading the mountain properly — looking at aspect, elevation, recent snowfall — and making decisions that serve the skiing.

Honest Matching

We're upfront about ability requirements for each trip. We'd rather turn someone away at the enquiry stage than have them struggle on the hill. It's better for them, better for the group.

No Bloat

We don't include stuff to look good on a brochure. The price covers what it covers. We're transparent about what's included and what isn't — and we don't invent activities to pad the week out.
