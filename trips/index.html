The Trips

Three resorts. Three windows. All run with the same standards — small groups, proper terrain, early starts.

Each trip runs for 7 nights and takes a maximum of 6 skiers. All are led personally — not delegated. The itinerary isn't fixed: we plan each day based on snowpack, aspect, and where the skiing is at its best.

Accommodation is included. Flights are not — you book your own travel to resort. Travel insurance with ski and off-piste cover is mandatory.

Limited Spaces

Verbier sits at the heart of the 4 Vallées — one of the largest interconnected ski areas in the Alps. Long known as one of Europe’s great ski destinations, the area offers an enormous amount of ground to explore. With much of the terrain above 2,700m, conditions remain reliable throughout the season.

We base ourselves in Verbier village and plan each day around where the mountain is skiing best — from the wide-open slopes above the village to long runs stretching across the valley towards Nendaz.

This trip suits skiers who are comfortable on red runs and enjoy long, rewarding days exploring the mountain as part of a small group.

Spaces Available

Val d'Isère in March is a different beast to the Christmas crush — snowpack is reliable, the days are longer, and Espace Killy is at its best. We use the full area: from the Bellevarde face to the Col de l'Iseran corridor and across into Tignes when the mood takes us.

This trip is suitable for confident intermediates who are ready to push their skiing. You'll be on red and black terrain all week, with off-piste sessions built in when conditions allow. If you're a strong blue / weak red skier, this isn't the right trip — but we'd encourage you to enquire and have an honest conversation.

Now Taking Interest

A high-altitude, ski-in / ski-out base designed for early starts and long days on the mountain.

Val Thorens offers reliable snow, fast lift access, and direct ski-in / ski-out accommodation — making it an ideal setting for a Clean Line Ski week. Days start early, flow naturally, and end with a simple ski back to the hotel, allowing recovery to happen without forcing the pace.

The terrain suits confident skiers who want to cover ground, maintain rhythm, and ski consistently well across the entire week.
