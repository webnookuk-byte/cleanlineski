The Trips

Two resorts. Two windows. Both run with the same standards — small groups, proper terrain, early starts.

Each trip runs for 7 nights and takes a maximum of 6 skiers. Both are led personally — not delegated. The itinerary isn't fixed: we plan each day based on snowpack, aspect, and where the skiing is at its best.

Accommodation is included. Flights are not — you book your own travel to resort. Travel insurance with ski and off-piste cover is mandatory.

Verbier sits at the heart of the 4 Vallées — one of the largest interconnected ski areas in the Alps. The terrain above 2,700m holds snow well and offers some of the most interesting off-piste in Europe. We base ourselves in Verbier village and plan each day around where the conditions are best: whether that's the Attelas face, the Col des Mines, or heading into Nendaz when the crowds thin out.

This trip suits skiers who are confident on all black runs and want to push into off-piste and bigger terrain under proper guidance. You don't need to be an expert — but you should be able to handle steep, variable snow and make sound decisions under pressure.

Val d'Isère in March is a different beast to the Christmas crush — snowpack is reliable, the days are longer, and Espace Killy is at its best. We use the full area: from the Bellevarde face to the Col de l'Iseran corridor and across into Tignes when the mood takes us.

This trip is suitable for confident intermediates who are ready to push their skiing. You'll be on red and black terrain all week, with off-piste sessions built in when conditions allow. If you're a strong blue / weak red skier, this isn't the right trip — but we'd encourage you to enquire and have an honest conversation.
